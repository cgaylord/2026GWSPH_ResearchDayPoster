    \begin{posterblock}{Background \& Objectives}
      Shotgun metagenomics and amplicon sequencing frequently yield
      discordant community profiles, commonly attributed to biological
      or wet-lab differences --- but how much arises from
      \textsl{computational pipeline} choices?

      \medskip
      We isolate the dry-lab contribution by routing shotgun data
      through the amplicon pipeline, holding wet-lab inputs constant.
      We ask whether Fisher-Rao distance detects pipeline-driven
      differences more reliably than Bray-Curtis.

      \textbf{Dataset:} P\'{e}rez-Losada et al.\ (2022) vermicomposting;

    \begin{posterblock}{Fisher-Rao Distance \& PERMANOVA Results}

      \small
      Fisher-Rao distance arises necessarily from the Fisher
      Information Matrix and is the \textsl{unique} metric on the
      probability simplex invariant under sufficient statistics
      ({\v C}encov, 1982). Bray-Curtis was adopted by historical
      convention, not derived from the multinomial model.

      \medskip
      \begin{minipage}[t]{0.44\linewidth}
        For $\mathbf{p}$, $\mathbf{q}$ over $K$ taxa:
        \[
          D_{\mathrm{FR}} = 2\sqrt{K}\arccos\!\left(\sum_{k=1}^K \sqrt{p_k q_k}\right)
        \]
      \end{minipage}%
      \hfill
      \begin{minipage}[t]{0.52\linewidth}
        \vspace{0.3ex}
        \textbf{PERMANOVA} (9,999 permutations, phylum level):\\[0.5ex]
        \begin{tabular}{llrr}\hline
          & & \multicolumn{2}{c}{$p$-value} \\
          Stream & Effect & FR & BC \\ \hline
          Amplicon  & Treatment & 0.004** & 0.007** \\
                    & Time      & \textbf{0.073.} & 0.190 \\[0.3ex]
          MEGAHIT   & Treatment & 0.0001*** & 0.0004*** \\
                    & Time      & 0.079. & 0.093. \\ \hline
        \end{tabular}\\[0.5ex]
        \textit{FR detects time effect ($p=0.073$) missed by BC.}
      \end{minipage}

    \begin{posterblock}{Conclusions \& Future Directions}
      Much reported shotgun-amplicon discordance reflects
      \textsl{dry-lab pipeline differences}.\\%, not biology.
      Routing shotgun reads through the amplicon pipeline
      recovers concordant community profiles.

      \medskip
      Fisher-Rao distance, derived necessarily from the Fisher
      Information Matrix of the multinomial distribution, provides
      more stable community comparisons than Bray-Curtis.

      \medskip
      Further study of the sampling distribution of Fisher-Rao
      distance will support power analysis and Type~I error
      characterization for PERMANOVA-based testing.

      \medskip
      Validation against mock communities with known composition
      will establish the accuracy of contig-based 16S extraction
      across sequencing platforms.
